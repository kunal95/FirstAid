Victim is responsive
1. Stand behind the victim.
2. Make a fist and place the thumb side of that hand against the victim’s abdomen, just above the navel and below the ribs. Grasp fist with the other hand.
3. Quickly thrust inward and upward into the abdomen.
4. Repeat thrusts until object is expelled or victim becomes unresponsive.
Victim is unresponsive
1. If needed, help the victim to the ground and alert emergency services.
2. Begin chest thrusts as you would with CPR. Each time the airway is opened look for the object in the victim’s throat and if you can see it, remove it – being careful not to lodge the object further into the victim’s throat.
3. Continue chest thrusts until EMS / Paramedics arrive, or the victim shows signs of breathing /responsiveness.
