Minor wounds include abrasions, lacerations, punctures and incisions. The most significant issues to consider with any open wound are control of bleeding and infection.
General Wound
1. If bleeding, apply direct pressure with a clean cloth or absorbent pad.
2. Wash area with antibacterial soap and clean until there appears to be no foreign matter in the wound.
3. Cover area with an adhesive bandage or gauze wrap.
Bruising
Caused by broken blood vessels leaking blood under the skin. Bruising can be minimal or large and severe.
1. Apply ice to injury to reduce pain, bleeding and swelling.
2. To prevent frost bite to the injured area, place a thin towel or cloth between the skin and ice. Limit ice application to 20 minutes on, 20 off.
Crush Injury
Occurs when a body part is subjected to a high degree of force or pressure . Example: smashed fingers in door.
1. Apply ice just as you would with a bruising injury.
2. If pain is severe and does not lessen with ice or there is decreased sensation, weakness, or paleness of the skin in the affected area, seek emergency care.
