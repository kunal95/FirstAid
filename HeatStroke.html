Heatstroke occurs if your body temperature continues to rise. At this point, emergency treatment is needed. In a period of hours, untreated heatstroke can cause damage to your brain, heart, kidneys and muscles. These injuries get worse the longer treatment is delayed, increasing your risk of very serious complications.
First Aid Interventions
1. If you think a person may be experiencing heatstroke, seek immediate medical help. Call your local emergency services number.
2. Help the person move to a shaded location and remove excess clothing.
3. Place ice packs or cold, wet towels on the person's head, neck, armpits and groin.
4. Mist the person with water while a fan is blowing on him/her.
