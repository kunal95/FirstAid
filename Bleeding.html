Bleeding is one of the most rectifiable causes of death following trauma, therefore controlling external bleeding is a main priority when administering care in a pre-hospital environment.
Direct pressure
1. Rip or cut away clothing so wound can be seen.
2. Place an absorbent pad directly over the wound.
3. Apply firm, direct pressure over the wound.
4. The victim can assist if they are able.
Pressure Bandage
1. Wrap a conforming bandage securely over the pad to maintain pressure and hold the gauze in place.
2. Bandage should be loose enough so a finger can slip under the bandage.
If Bleeding Continues
1. As the first dressings become soaked with blood, apply more pads, dressings and maintain firm, direct pressure.
2. Do not remove the first dressings, just continue to add more if soaking through continues.
Elevation
Elevate the affected area above the level of the heart, if possible
Tourniquet
An improvised tourniquet is only to be used as last resort. Do not remove once applied.
Managing Shock
1. Ensure an open airway for the victim and adequate breathing.
2. Keep the bleeding under control.
3. Prevent chilling or overheating.
4. Keep victim lying flat with feet slightly elevated if possible.
